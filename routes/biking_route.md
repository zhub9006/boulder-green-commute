# 🚴 Biking Route: Detailed Guide

**Address:** 45 Oak Avenue → 1800 Broadway  
**Total Distance:** 4.03 km (2.5 mi)  
**Total Time:** ~16 minutes  
**Elevation:** Flat (~15 m total gain)  
**Calories:** ~80 kcal

---

## Turn-by-Turn Directions

### 1. Start: 45 Oak Avenue
📍 Head **south** on Oak Avenue  
🚴 96 m | ~7 seconds

> Oak Avenue is a quiet residential street. This is the shortest and most peaceful segment. The road has a bike lane and minimal traffic.

**🏠 Distance Remaining:** 3.93 km

---

### 2. Turn Right onto 19th Street
📍 Turn **west** (right) onto 19th Street  
🚴 2,157 m | ~1.4 min

> Turn onto 19th Street and use the designated bike lane. This is the longest and most exposed segment of the ride (1.4 km), so stay in the bike lane, watch for right-hook turns at every intersection.

**🤝 Landmarks:**
- CU Boulder area
- Several Colorado-style brewery homes
- Sunset views over the Flatirons if heading west

**📌 Safety:** 19th is the busiest road. Stay alert. Lights at every crossing. Watch for buses and delivery trucks turning right across the bike lane.

**🏠 Distance Remaining:** 1.773 km

---

### 3. Turn Right onto 20th Street
📍 Turn **north** (right) at 20th St  
🚴 672 m | ~26 seconds

> 20th Street is quieter and allows cyclists well.

**🤝 Landmarks:**
- University Hill neighborhood
- Local bookstores and cafes

**📌 Safety:** Residential street. Watch for pedestrians and pets.

**🏠 Distance Remaining:** 1.101 km

---

### 4. Turn Left onto Pearl Street
📍 Turn **west** (left) onto Pearl Street (Pedestrian Mall)  
🚴 216 m | ~8.5 seconds

> Pearl Street Mall is a pedestrian zone. You MUST walk your bike for this short segment. Use the bike racks at Walnut Street.

**🤝 Landmarks:**
- Pearl Street Mall with local boutiques
- Rio Grande, Japango restaurants nearby

**📌 Safety:** Dismount and walk your bike on the mall path. Very pedestrian-heavy. Bike lock-ups at Walnut St.

**🏠 Distance Remaining:** 884 m

---

### 5. Turn Left onto 18th Street
📍 Turn **north** (left) at 18th St  
🚴 249 m | ~10 seconds

> Short residential stretch. Quiet and easy.

**🏠 Distance Remaining:** 635 m

---

### 6. Turn Right onto Canyon Boulevard
📍 Turn **east** (right) onto Canyon Boulevard — the scenic highlight  
🚴 637 m | ~25.4 seconds

> **THIS IS THE GEM.** Canyon Boulevard has a **physically separated bike lane** along Boulder Creek. This is one of Boulder 's premier cycling corridors.

**🤝 Landmarks:**
- Boulder Creek — turquoise, scenic river views
- Boulder Falls (short detour)
- Cottonwood shade trees

**📌 Safety:** Dedicated bike lane with barriers. Watch for pedestrians/joggers on the adjacent path. Enjoy the ride — this is the best part!

**🏠 Distance Remaining:** 0 m

---

### Arrive: 1800 Broadway
📍 Junction near Broadway. 1800 Broadway is just east along Broadway from the Canyon Blvd intersection.

## Seasonal Tips for Bikers

### 🌸 Spring (April–May)
- Best biking weather in Boulder!
- Wildflowers along Canyon Blvd
- Mornings may be chilly (50°F) — bring a vest or arm warmers
- Creek is high; watch for spray on the path

### ☀️ Summer (June–August)
- Warm and dry; 75–85°F
- Canyon Blvd gets crowded — ride early or late
- UV is intense at 5,400 ft elevation — sunscreen + sunglasses
- Thunderstorms in late afternoon — plan your ride earlier

### 🍂 Fall (September–November)
- Peak color along the creek — golden aspens
- Cool mornings (45°F); afternoons perfect
- Racor bike trail closure on certain days — check closures
- Rain is possible; fenders recommended

### ❄️ Winter (December–March)
- Ice is the main hazard — studded tires or walk your bike
- Canyon Blvd path may have ice patches — walk when icy
- Snow removal is good on 19th St; slower on others
- Dress in layers; wind chill off the creek
- Average high: 45°F; low: 22°F
