# 🌿 Green Commuting Tips & Seasonal Guide

**Seasonal advice, weather prep, gear recommendations, and Boulder-specific resources for sustainable commuting.**

---

## Quick Seasonal Calendar

### 🌸 Spring (April–May)
- **Weather:** 45–65°F, variable, chance of rain
- **Best segments:** Canyon Blvd is gorgeous with wildflowers
- **Gear:** Light rain jacket, bike fenders (spring rains!), sunglasses
- **Tip:** Last frost usually mid-May; avoid biking on icy patches in early April

### ☀️ Summer (June–August)
- **Weather:** 75–95°F, dry, intense UV
- **Best segments:** Canyon Blvd for swimming/wading access at Creek
- **Gear:** Sunscreen (SPF 50+ at altitude!), UV-blocking sunglasses, water bottle
- **Tip:** Thunderstorms typically 2–5 PM — ride earlier in the morning

### 🍂 Fall (September–November)
- **Weather:** 40–70°F, peak foliage in October
- **Best segments:** Entire route — golden aspens on Canyon Blvd are spectacular
- **Gear:** Layers (morning cold, afternoon warm), reflective gear for shorter days
- **Tip:** October foliage = best commute views of the year. Bring a camera!

### ❄️ Winter (December–March)
- **Weather:** 20–45°F, possible snow/ice
- **Best segments:** 19th St (cleared first), avoid shady spots on Oak Ave
- **Gear:** Studded bike tires, thermal layers, insulated gloves, balaclava
- **Tip:** When temps drop below 25°F or ice is present, **walk your bike** on Canyon Blvd. Safe > fast.

## Essential Gear for Bikers

| Item | Recommendation | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Helmet** | Bontrager / Giro | Essential — Colorado law requires it |
| **Lights** | Knog Blinder + Seeq rear | 360° visibility at dawn/dusk |
| **Fenders** | SKS fenders | Keep mud/water off clothes in spring/fall |

## Weather-Appropriate Riding Strategies

| Condition | Strategy |
|---|---|
| Light rain (spring/fall) | Wear cycling over-layer, bike cautiously, reduced tire pressure for grip |
| Heavy rain/thunderstorm | **Walk the bike** until it passes. 2 min wait vs. dangerous crash |
| Snow/ice (winter) | Walk bike on Canyon Blvd; ride 19th St only if plowed |
| Hot summer sun | Early morning ride (before 9 AM), hydrate with 500ml water before start |
| Windy (common in Boulder) | E-bike helps with headwinds; use lower gears on 19th St approach |
| Sub-freezing | Studded tires or transit (RTD) — safety first |

## Boulder-Specific Resources

### Bike Infrastructure
- **Canyon Boulevard Path:** The #1 cycling corridor in Boulder. Separated bike lane, maintained year-round.
- **19th Street Bike Route:** Designated through the city; watch for right turns at intersections.
- **Bike Boulder:** Local shop for rentals, tune-ups, and guidance — (303) 443-9354
- **B-Cycle:** Boulder bike-share system. Stations at Walnut/Broadway and University Hill area.

### Transit
- **RTD Bus:** Routes 1, 3, 7, 10 on 19th Street. Use the RTDA app for real-time tracking.
- **RTD Outrider:** Demand-responsive micro-transit to fill last-mile gaps
- **EcoPass:** Many Boulder employers subsidize RTD passes ($20/month)
- **Commuteride:** RTD commuter matching service

### Incentives
- **Colorado E-Bike Rebate:** $1,200 for Class 1 e-bikes
- **Boulder Climate Action Plan (CAP):** 80% transport GHG reduction by 2050
- **Bike to Work Day:** Annual June event — free breakfasts at RTD hubs
- **B-Cycle Referral:** Refer a friend and get free ride credits

## Building a Habit: 21-Day Challenge

### Week 1: Test (Days 1–7)
- Bike 1 day, Drive 4 days. Get comfortable with the route.
- Note which segments feel safe and which need caution.
- Rate your comfort level (1–10) at the end of each ride.

### Week 2: Build (Days 8–14)
- Bike 2 days/week. Standardize your day (e.g., Tue/Thu).
- Prepare gear the night before.

### Week 3: Accumulate (Days 15–21)
- Bike 3 days/week. Add a walking day for variety.
- Try B-Cycle — same path, different feel.
- Track your savings.

### Week 4+: Commit (Day 22+)
- Bike 4+ days/week. Make it a non-negotiable routine.
- Share progress on social media — inspire others!
